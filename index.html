What can fast food tell us about Sydney?

If you want to know a city, try some of the local fast food. Sydney is home to a seemingly endless variety of fast food. Australians spend on average $311 per annum on burgers, fries, pizza, and kebabs. But from Bondi to Mt Druitt, the greasiness, price, availability, marketing and taste of fast food varies. So what can the distribution of fast food restaurants tell us about Sydney-siders? A lot, it turns out.

My interest in answering this question began when Twitter user @THE_REAL_BIGJEZ tweeted a map of Sydney's "Red Rooster line" dividing western Sydney from the east.
